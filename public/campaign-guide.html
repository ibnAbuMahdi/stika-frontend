Campaign Creation Guide

Campaign Planning Essentials

Define Your Objectives

  • Brand Awareness: Maximize visibility in target areas
  • Event Promotion: Drive attendance to specific events
  • Product Launch: Introduce new products to local markets
  • Location Marketing: Promote stores, restaurants, services

Know Your Audience

  • Demographics: Age, income, lifestyle of target customers
  • Geographic Patterns: Where your audience lives, works, shops
  • Traffic Flow: Peak movement times and popular routes
  • Seasonal Behavior: Market days, school terms, festival periods

Step-by-Step Campaign Creation

1. Campaign Setup

  • Campaign Name: Use descriptive names (e.g., "Lagos Island Office Launch Q1 2024")
  • Client Selection: Choose from your existing clients or send invite code to your client
  • Campaign Type: Choose template or custom configuration
  • Duration: Set realistic timeframes (minimum 7 days recommended)

2. Geofence Strategy

High-Impact Locations:

Commercial Areas
Markets, shopping centers, business districts
Transport Hubs
Bus stops, taxi ranks, major intersections
Educational Zones
Schools, universities, training centers
Residential Areas
Dense housing estates, apartment complexes

Geofence Best Practices:

  • Size: 5KM-10KM radius for focused areas, >20KM for broad coverage
  • Shape: Use regular shapes to make it easier for riders to navigate.
  • Overlap: Slight overlap between geofences ensures continuous coverage
  • Exclusions: Avoid government buildings, restricted zones, low-traffic areas

3. Visual Assets Preparation

Sticker Design Requirements:

  • Format: PNG or JPG, minimum 300 DPI
  • Size: Optimized for tricycle side panels (standard dimensions)
  • Contrast: High contrast colors for visibility
  • Text: Large, readable fonts (minimum 24pt)
  • Branding: Include logo, contact info, call-to-action

Brand Guidelines:

  • Consistent Colors: Match your brand palette
  • Clear Messaging: Simple, direct value proposition
  • Local Language: Consider Yoruba, Igbo, Hausa for local markets
  • Cultural Sensitivity: Respect local customs and preferences

4. Scheduling & Timing

Duration Planning:

  • Short Campaigns: 7-14 days for events, flash sales
  • Medium Campaigns: 30-60 days for product launches
  • Long Campaigns: 90+ days for brand building

5. Budget Allocation

Pricing Factors:

  • Location Premium: CBD areas cost 2-3x suburban rates
  • Duration Discounts: Longer campaigns get better rates
  • Volume Discounts: Multiple geofences reduce per-unit costs

Budget Planning:

  • Total Campaign Budget: Include 10-15% buffer for overruns
  • Performance Buffers: Reserve funds for high-performing geofences
  • Testing Budget: Allocate 20% for A/B testing different areas

Campaign Optimization Tips

Monitor Key Metrics:

  • Coverage Rate: Percentage of geofence area covered daily
  • Dwell Time: Average time riders spend in target areas
  • Frequency: How often the same areas are covered
  • Cost Efficiency: Cost per hour of verified coverage

Troubleshooting Common Issues:

  • Low Rider Participation: Increase rates or improve geofence locations
  • Poor Coverage: Add more geofences or increase session duration
  • Quality Issues: Tighten verification requirements and fleet captain oversight
